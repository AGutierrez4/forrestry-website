# Homepage Copy Rewrite — RLM Analysis

**Problem Statement**: The current forrestry.ai homepage copy is competent but generic. It reads like any AI SaaS tool. It doesn't carry the founder's story, the emotional weight of the 80-hour realization, or the specific pain that the target audience (funnel builders, online entrepreneurs, people who have tried and stalled) actually feels. The tagline, hero, problem section, founder bio, and CTAs all need to be elevated to match the depth of the origin story and the specificity of the audience.

**Goal**: Rewrite all homepage copy to resonate deeply with the target audience (Russell Brunson's ecosystem, funnel builders, online entrepreneurs who have stalled) while keeping enough story back for the webinar. No layout or structural changes. Copy only.

---

## Phase 1: Audience Analysis

### Who is landing on this page?
- Entrepreneurs who have tried to build a funnel and hit the wall
- People who know what a webinar funnel is, what a squeeze page is, what an email sequence is
- People who have used AI tools (ChatGPT, ClickFunnels AI, Jasper) and been disappointed by the output
- People who have bought courses, joined programs, maybe even completed One Funnel Away Challenge, but still haven't launched
- People who feel like imposters staring at a blank page
- People who are paying for multiple tools (funnel builder + email platform + ad tool + AI writer + design tool) and still doing all the work themselves

### What do they feel right now?
- Frustrated: they know what they need to build but can't get it done
- Overwhelmed: the stack of assets required for a real launch is massive
- Skeptical: they've been promised "easy" before and it wasn't
- Guilty: they have a real offer and a real audience waiting, but they keep stalling
- Alone: they're doing this by themselves and nobody around them understands

### What would make them stop scrolling?
- Seeing their exact situation described back to them
- Specificity (not "build funnels faster" but "the squeeze page, the webinar script, the 50-slide deck, the email sequences, the ad copy")
- Honesty (not hype, not "magic button," not "5 minutes to a funnel")
- A founder who has clearly been where they are

## Phase 2: Current Copy Audit

### Tagline: "Do more. Build less. Launch faster."
**Problem**: Generic. Could belong to any productivity tool. Doesn't speak to funnels, doesn't speak to the pain, doesn't differentiate. It's a fine tagline for a mature brand with recognition. For a pre-launch brand that nobody knows yet, it says nothing.

### Hero Eyebrow: "For Entrepreneurs Who Are Done Waiting"
**Problem**: Vague. "Done waiting" for what? It doesn't name the specific frustration.

### Hero Subheadline: "You have the idea. You have the drive. The only thing standing between you and a live, revenue-generating funnel is the friction. We remove it."
**Problem**: "Friction" is abstract. The audience doesn't think in terms of "friction." They think in terms of "I've been staring at this blank page for three weeks" and "I don't know what to write for my webinar script" and "I'm paying $297/month for tools I barely use."

### Problem Section Body: "Tools like ClickFunnels hand you the bricks and tell you to build a house."
**Problem**: Good metaphor, but names ClickFunnels directly. Per Andrew's guidance, we should remove the brand mention. The audience knows exactly which tools we're talking about without naming them.

### Pain Point Cards
**Problem**: Decent but generic. "You spend hours on AI prompts that produce nothing usable" is good. But the cards don't escalate. They don't build toward the real cost, which is that you never launch.

### Founder Section
**Problem**: Too short and too vague. "I was you. Great idea, real drive, and a graveyard of half-built funnels to show for it." That's one sentence of resonance followed by generic product positioning. The origin story has SO much more to draw from. We need enough to create connection and curiosity without giving away the full webinar story.

### CTA Copy: "Claim Founding Member Access"
**Problem**: This works for the funnel/webinar flow. But on the homepage during launch phase, the primary action should drive toward the webinar (per our earlier strategic discussion). Keeping this for now since Andrew hasn't confirmed the CTA pivot yet, but flagging it.

## Phase 3: Copy Recommendations

### TAGLINE (used in footer, meta, brand)

**Current**: "Do more. Build less. Launch faster."

**Recommended options** (pick one):

1. "Your funnel. Finally finished."
   - Why: Speaks directly to the graveyard of half-built funnels. "Finally" carries the weight of years of trying.

2. "From idea to launch. No blank pages."
   - Why: "Blank page" is the universal enemy in this audience. Names the before (idea) and after (launch) clearly.

3. "The 80-hour funnel stack. Built in one conversation."
   - Why: Uses the specific number from the origin story. Creates immediate curiosity. "One conversation" is the Brain Dump differentiator.

4. "Stop building. Start launching."
   - Why: Short, punchy, reframes the problem. The audience has been "building" forever. They need to start "launching."

My recommendation: Option 3 or Option 1. Option 3 is bolder and more specific. Option 1 is cleaner and more emotional.

### HERO SECTION

**Current eyebrow**: "For Entrepreneurs Who Are Done Waiting"
**Recommended**: "YOU HAVE THE OFFER. YOU HAVE THE AUDIENCE. NOW FINISH THE FUNNEL."
- Why: Three short punches. Names what they already have (validating them) and names what's missing (the funnel). More specific than "done waiting."

**Current H1**: "Do more. Build less. / Launch faster."
**Recommended**: "Your entire funnel. / Built from one conversation."
- Why: "Entire funnel" is specific (not "do more," which is vague). "One conversation" is the Brain Dump differentiator and it's intriguing. It makes people ask "how?" which is exactly what you want.
- Alt option: "Eighty hours of funnel work. / One conversation." — bolder, uses the number, but might need the context that comes later on the page.

**Current subheadline**: "You have the idea. You have the drive. The only thing standing between you and a live, revenue-generating funnel is the friction. We remove it."
**Recommended**: "The squeeze page. The webinar script. The email sequences. The ad copy. The entire launch stack that used to take weeks of writing, designing, and second-guessing. Built for you, in order, from a single Brain Dump conversation."
- Why: Lists the specific assets (the audience knows exactly what these are). "Used to take weeks" validates their experience. "In order" is a key Funnel Studio differentiator. "Brain Dump conversation" introduces the mechanism.

**Current social proof line**: "Founding member spots are limited. No credit card required to apply."
**Recommended**: "Founding member beta. Limited spots. No credit card required."
- Why: Tighter. "Beta" signals early access and exclusivity. Same info, fewer words.

### PROBLEM SECTION

**Current label**: "THE PROBLEM"
**Keep as-is.**

**Current H2**: "You don't have a funnel problem. / You have a friction problem."
**Recommended**: "You know exactly what you need to build. / That was never the problem."
- Why: Validates them first (you're not dumb, you know the strategy). Then reframes: the problem isn't knowledge, it's execution. This is more specific to the audience than "friction problem" which is abstract.

**Current body**: "Tools like ClickFunnels hand you the bricks and tell you to build a house..."
**Recommended**: "You have the offer. You know your audience. You could probably sketch the funnel on a napkin right now. But then you sit down to actually build it, and the real work starts. The long-form squeeze page. The 60-minute webinar script. The slide deck. The email sequences. The social ads. The promo videos. Every single asset has to be written, designed, and tested before you ever see a dollar. That is not a strategy problem. That is a system problem."
- Why: Draws directly from the origin story's 80-hour breakdown without giving away the full narrative. Lists the specific assets (the audience will nod at every single one). Reframes from "you need to learn more" to "the system is broken." Removes the ClickFunnels brand mention.

**Current pain point cards**:
1. ⏳ You spend hours on AI prompts that produce nothing usable
2. 🧩 Your tools don't talk to each other, so you become the glue
3. 💸 You're paying for 6 platforms to do what one should
4. 🔥 You burn out before your funnel ever goes live

**Recommended** (rewritten to be more specific and escalating):
1. ⏳ "You open the AI tool, hit generate, and the output reads like it was written for nobody"
2. 📝 "You have 6 tabs open, 3 subscriptions running, and still no launch date"
3. 😶 "You know what a high-converting funnel looks like. You just can't get yours to look like that."
4. 🔥 "You start to wonder if maybe this just is not for you. (It is.)"

- Why: Card 1 is the "gut sink" moment from the origin story (heart hit the floor when the AI output generated). Card 2 is the tool sprawl problem. Card 3 is the imposter gap (knowing vs doing). Card 4 is the emotional low point, with the parenthetical reassurance that mirrors Andrew's voice: "I knew it was for them. Because it was for me."

### SOLUTION / PRODUCTS SECTION

**Current H2**: "Your funnel. Built in the right order."
**Recommended**: "One conversation. Every asset you need to launch."
- Why: "One conversation" is the differentiator. "Every asset" is specific. "To launch" is the outcome they want.

**Current subheadline**: "Funnel Studio doesn't hand you a blank page. It walks you through every stage of your launch, in sequence, so nothing falls through the cracks."
**Recommended**: "You talk. Funnel Studio builds. Squeeze page, webinar script, email sequences, ad copy, promo scripts. Stage by stage, in the right order, so nothing gets missed and nothing gets skipped."
- Why: "You talk. Funnel Studio builds." is the simplest possible explanation of the Brain Dump mechanism. Then it lists the specific assets again (repetition is intentional, this is what the audience cares about).

**Funnel Studio card tagline**: "Your entire funnel. Built stage by stage."
**Recommended**: "Talk for 15 minutes. Launch with everything."
- Why: "15 minutes" is specific and almost unbelievable (in a good way). Creates curiosity.

**Funnel Studio card body**: Keep mostly as-is but remove "Brain Dump your idea once" (save the Brain Dump explanation for the How It Works section) and replace with: "Your webinar script, registration page, email sequences, and ad copy. Built in order, from your story, your offer, and your audience. No blank pages. No guessing what comes next."

**Answer Your Audience and Website Builder cards**: Keep as-is. They're coming soon products and don't need the same depth.

### HOW IT WORKS SECTION

**Current H2**: "Three steps to your first launch."
**Keep as-is.** This is clean and clear.

**Step 1 — Brain Dump**
**Current**: "Tell Funnel Studio your idea, your audience, and your offer. No prompts to engineer. No frameworks to learn. Just talk."
**Recommended**: "Tell Funnel Studio your story, your offer, and who you serve. No prompts to engineer. No templates to fill out. Just a conversation."
- Why: "Your story" is more personal than "your idea." "Who you serve" is more specific than "your audience." "Templates to fill out" is a dig at every other tool they've tried.

**Step 2 — Build**
**Current**: "Watch your funnel take shape, stage by stage. Webinar script. Registration page. Email sequence. Ad copy. In order, every time."
**Recommended**: "Funnel Studio builds your entire launch stack. Squeeze page. Webinar script. Slide deck. Email sequences. Social ads. Promo scripts. Stage by stage, in the right order."
- Why: Adds the full asset list (slide deck, promo scripts) which the audience knows is part of a real launch. "In the right order" reinforces the key differentiator.

**Step 3 — Launch**
**Current**: "Go live with a complete, conversion-ready funnel. In hours, not months."
**Recommended**: "Go live with a complete funnel ecosystem. Not a template. Not a starting point. A launch-ready system built from your words."
- Why: "Not a template. Not a starting point." directly addresses their skepticism (every other tool gives them a template and calls it done). "Built from your words" ties back to the Brain Dump.

---

### FOUNDER SECTION

**Current label**: "WHY WE BUILT THIS"
**Recommended**: "THE FOUNDER"

**Current H2**: "You've been doing this alone long enough."
**Recommended**: "I built the tool I wish existed when I walked away."
- Why: "Walked away" is a hook. It creates curiosity (walked away from what?). It's honest. And it directly references the origin story without telling it.

**Current bio**: Generic "I was you" paragraph.
**Recommended**: "I found the funnel world in 2019 and the learning curve stopped me cold. The copy, the design, the tech, the strategy. All of it, at once. I walked away and spent the next several years earning my stripes in marketing with global brands, local businesses, and everyone in between. When I came back in 2025, ready to launch my own offer, I sat down with the best AI tools available and felt my heart sink. The output was thin. Generic. And I was still staring down weeks of real work. So I stopped building my funnel and started building the tool that should have existed already."
- Why: This is a compressed version of the origin story. It hits the three key beats (found funnels, walked away, came back and AI disappointed him, built the tool instead) without the full emotional arc that's reserved for the webinar. It's enough to create connection ("I've been there") and curiosity ("what did he build?") without spoiling the live story.

**Closing line**:
**Current**: "We're not just building tools. We're building the Forresters. A community of founders who actually launch."
**Recommended**: "This is not a magic button. It takes real work from you. But the 80 hours of writing, designing, and second-guessing? That part is over."
- Why: This is the honesty beat from the origin story. "Not a magic button" builds trust with a skeptical audience. "80 hours" drops the number naturally. "That part is over" is the promise. It also answers question #6 — yes, the honesty should be on the homepage. This audience has been oversold by everyone. The moment you say "this is not a magic button," you become the first person in this space who isn't lying to them. That's how you earn the click.

### CLOSING CTA SECTION

**Current H2**: "This is your moment."
**Current accent**: "Don't build it alone."
**Recommended H2**: "You have the offer. You have the audience."
**Recommended accent**: "Now finish the funnel."
- Why: Mirrors the eyebrow from the hero (bookending the page). "Finish the funnel" is the most direct, emotionally resonant CTA for this audience. They've started a dozen times. They need to finish.

**Current body**: "Founding member spots are limited. Lock in lifetime access at beta pricing before we open to the public."
**Recommended**: "Founding member beta is open. Limited spots. Lifetime access at founding pricing for the people who show up first."
- Why: "People who show up first" rewards action. "Founding pricing" is cleaner than "beta pricing."

**Current sub-copy**: "No credit card required. Apply in 60 seconds."
**Keep as-is.**
