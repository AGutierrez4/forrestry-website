# Copy Style Rules — Forrestry.ai / Funnel Studio

**Purpose**: Enforced writing rules for all user-facing copy on the website, emails, and marketing content.  
**Last Updated**: May 2, 2026

---

## Banned Punctuation

- **No em dashes (—)** in user-facing copy. Ever. Use commas, periods, colons, semicolons, or parentheses instead.
- En dashes (–) are acceptable only in number ranges (e.g., "$29–$59/mo", "4–5 tools").

## Banned Copy Patterns

- **No "it's not X, it's Y" contrast framing.** Don't write "You don't have a funnel problem. You have a friction problem." State the point directly instead.
- **No value bloat.** Don't compare a $47/mo tool to $25,000 in professional services. Compare against what the buyer is actually doing (DIY AI tool stack, not hiring a team of specialists).

## Preferred Alternatives to Em Dashes

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "A guided conversation — then it organizes..." | "A guided conversation that organizes..." or "A guided conversation, then it organizes..." |
| "Complete funnel — not disconnected pieces" | "Complete funnel instead of disconnected pieces" or "Complete funnel. Not disconnected pieces." |
| "Cancel anytime — no contracts" | "Cancel anytime. No contracts." |
| "General AI — you write the prompts" | "General AI. You write the prompts." |

## General Copy Voice

- Clear, direct, short sentences
- Active voice
- "You" and "your" focused
- Confident but not arrogant
- No corporate jargon
- No exclamation points in body copy (headlines are fine sparingly)
