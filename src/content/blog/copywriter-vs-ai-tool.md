## What You Get When You Hire a Copywriter

A professional funnel copywriter is a human expert who writes conversion-focused content for a living. Here is what that looks like in practice.

### The Process

1. **Discovery Call** - 60 to 90 minutes where the copywriter interviews you about your offer, audience, positioning, and sales process
2. **Research Phase** - The copywriter reviews your existing content, analyzes competitors, and sometimes conducts customer interviews
3. **Strategic Brief** - You receive a document outlining the messaging framework, core promise, objection handling strategy, and funnel structure
4. **First Draft** - 2 to 4 weeks later, you receive the full funnel copy (registration page, webinar/VSL script, sales page, email sequence)
5. **Revisions** - 2 to 4 rounds of edits based on your feedback
6. **Final Delivery** - Polished, ready-to-use copy

### What You Actually Receive

- **Registration page copy** (headline, subhead, bullet points, CTA)
- **Webinar script or VSL outline** (45 to 60 minutes, following a proven framework like Perfect Webinar)
- **Four-page sales letter** (headline, story, offer, close)
- **Email sequence** (8 to 15 emails: pre-event reminders, post-event follow-up, deadline emails)
- **Ad copy** (sometimes included, sometimes extra)
- **Strategic consultation** (the copywriter becomes a sounding board for positioning and offer decisions)

### The Quality Bar

A skilled copywriter brings:
- Deep knowledge of conversion psychology and frameworks
- Experience writing for your specific market (health, finance, B2B, coaching, etc.)
- Ability to extract your unique voice through interviews and editing
- Strategic input on offer structure, pricing, and funnel architecture
- Human intuition about what will resonate with your audience

The copy is custom. It is written for your offer, your audience, and your voice. When it is good, it feels like you wrote it, just better.

### The Investment

- **Entry-level copywriter:** $3,500 to $6,000, 3 to 6 weeks
- **Mid-level copywriter:** $6,000 to $10,000, 4 to 8 weeks
- **Expert copywriter:** $10,000 to $15,000+, 6 to 10 weeks (includes deeper research, more revisions, ongoing consulting)

## When to Hire a Copywriter

Here is when a professional copywriter is the better choice. Be honest with yourself about whether these apply.

### 1. You Have a Complex, High-Ticket Offer

If you are selling a $10,000+ program, a mastermind, or a done-for-you service, the nuance and strategic positioning required may be beyond what an AI tool can deliver. A skilled copywriter will dig into the specific objections, buying psychology, and decision-making process of high-ticket buyers in a way that frameworks alone cannot replicate.

### 2. Your Market Is Highly Regulated or Requires Extreme Compliance

If you are in health, finance, legal, or any space with strict advertising rules, you need a human who understands the compliance landscape and can write copy that converts without crossing lines. AI tools can generate compliant-sounding copy, but they cannot interpret regulatory gray areas the way an experienced human can.

### 3. You Want a Strategic Partner, Not Just a Tool

A great copywriter is not just a writer. They are a strategic consultant who challenges your positioning, refines your offer, and helps you think through the entire customer journey. If you value that level of collaboration and want someone to push back on your ideas, hire a human.

### 4. You Are Building One Major Funnel and Have the Budget and Time

If this is your one big launch for the year, you have $8,000 to $12,000 to invest, and you can wait 6 to 8 weeks, a professional copywriter is worth it. The ROI on that one funnel will justify the investment, and you will get copy that is custom-built for your exact audience.

### 5. Your Voice and Brand Are Extremely Specific

If your brand voice is a core differentiator (like a comedian selling a course, a provocative thought leader, or a highly personal brand), you may find it easier to work with a human who can nail that voice from the start rather than editing AI output to get it right.

Here is when Funnel Studio is the better choice.

If you are weeks away from a launch, or you need to test an offer quickly, waiting 6 weeks for a copywriter is not an option. Funnel Studio gets you launch-ready content in 1 to 3 days. You can test, iterate, and improve based on real data instead of waiting for perfection.

This is the sweet spot. You have enough revenue that DIY is a waste of your time, but not enough that hiring a $12,000 copywriter for every funnel makes sense. Funnel Studio gives you professional frameworks without the professional price tag.

## The Scenarios: Which Path Fits Your Situation?

Let's make this concrete with real examples.

### Scenario 1: First-Time Funnel Builder, $8K/Month Revenue

**Profile:** You are a coach or consultant doing $8K/month. You have never built a funnel before. You want to launch a webinar funnel for a $2,000 program.

**Recommendation:** Funnel Studio.

**Why:** You do not have $8,000 to spend on a copywriter, and you cannot afford to wait 8 weeks. Use Funnel Studio to generate your first funnel, launch it, and learn what works. Once you are doing $25K/month from that funnel, reinvest in a professional copywriter to level it up.

---

### Scenario 2: Established Business, $50K/Month, One Major Launch Per Year

**Profile:** You are an established course creator doing $50K/month. You launch one major funnel per year, and it generates $200K+ in sales.

**Recommendation:** Hire a professional copywriter.

**Why:** The ROI on a $12,000 copywriter is obvious if that funnel generates $200K. You have the budget, the timeline, and the need for strategic depth. A great copywriter will refine your positioning, handle objections you did not think of, and deliver custom copy worth the investment.

---

### Scenario 3: Scaling Fast, Need 5+ Funnels in the Next 6 Months

**Profile:** You are a service provider with multiple offers (1:1 coaching, group program, course, done-for-you). You need funnels for all of them.

**Recommendation:** Funnel Studio.

**Why:** Paying $8,000 per funnel for five funnels is $40,000. That is not realistic for most businesses. Funnel Studio lets you build all five for a few hundred dollars per month. Use the same Blueprint, customize the content for each offer, and launch them all in a few weeks instead of a few months.

---

### Scenario 4: High-Ticket ($10K+) Offer, Complex Sale

**Profile:** You are selling a $15,000 mastermind or done-for-you service. The sales process involves discovery calls, applications, and nuanced objection handling.

**Recommendation:** Hire a professional copywriter.

**Why:** High-ticket sales require messaging sophistication that frameworks alone cannot deliver. A skilled copywriter who has sold high-ticket offers before will understand the psychology, pacing, and proof elements you need. This is not the place to save money.

---

### Scenario 5: Testing a New Offer, Not Sure It Will Work Yet

**Profile:** You have an idea for a new offer but have not validated it yet. You want to test it with a simple funnel before committing to a full launch.

**Recommendation:** Funnel Studio.

**Why:** Do not spend $8,000 on copy for an unvalidated offer. Use Funnel Studio to build a test funnel in a few days, run some traffic, and see if people respond. If it works, reinvest in professional copy. If it does not, you just saved yourself $8,000.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes. And a lot of smart marketers do.

Here is a hybrid approach that works:

1. **Use Funnel Studio to build your first version** - Get it live in 1 to 3 days
2. **Launch and collect data** - Run traffic, see what converts, identify weak spots
3. **Hire a copywriter to refine the high-leverage pieces** - Maybe the webinar is working but the sales page is weak. Hire a copywriter to rewrite just that one piece.

This gives you speed (you launched fast), data (you know what needs fixing), and strategic investment (you only pay a copywriter to improve what matters).

You can also use Funnel Studio for secondary funnels (lead magnets, tripwires, evergreen sequences) and save your copywriter budget for your one big annual launch.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### If I use Funnel Studio, will my funnel sound like everyone else's?

No, for two reasons. First, Funnel Studio generates content based on YOUR Strategic Blueprint (your offer, audience, positioning, origin story). That is unique to you. Second, you edit the output to add your voice, stories, and examples. The final version will not sound like anyone else because you customized it. What you DO share with other Funnel Studio users is the underlying framework (Perfect Webinar structure, StoryBrand principles, etc.). But frameworks are not voice. You bring the voice.

### Can a copywriter use Funnel Studio to speed up their process?

Absolutely. Some copywriters use Funnel Studio to generate first drafts, then refine them. It cuts their writing time in half, which means they can take on more clients or charge less. If you are hiring a copywriter, ask if they use AI tools as part of their process. Smart ones do.

### What if I hire a copywriter and the funnel does not convert?

This is a real risk. Copywriters write based on the information you give them. If your offer is not validated, your positioning is unclear, or your traffic is cold, even great copy will struggle. Before hiring a copywriter, make sure you have sold your offer at least a few times manually (in DMs, on sales calls, etc.). That proves the offer works and gives the copywriter something real to build from.

### Can Funnel Studio write as well as a human copywriter?

Not out of the box, no. The first draft from Funnel Studio will be strategically sound (it follows proven frameworks), but it will not have your voice, your stories, or your specific examples. You need to edit it. Think of Funnel Studio like a junior copywriter who knows the frameworks but needs your input to make it great. A professional copywriter delivers a more polished first draft with less editing required.

### If I start with Funnel Studio, can I switch to a copywriter later?

Yes, and this is a smart path for a lot of businesses. Use Funnel Studio to get version 1.0 live, test it, see what works, and then hire a copywriter to rewrite the high-leverage pieces (like the webinar or sales page). You will give the copywriter better input because you will have real data about what is working and what is not.

### How do I know if I am in the revenue range where Funnel Studio makes sense?

If you are doing $5,000 to $50,000 per month, Funnel Studio is the sweet spot. Below $5K, you may not have the budget for either (DIY might be your only option). Above $50K, you can afford professional copywriters and should consider them for your major launches.
