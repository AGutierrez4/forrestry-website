Here is the truth nobody in the funnel space wants to say out loud: the cost of building a funnel is not one number. It depends on which path you take, how much your time is worth, and how long you are willing to wait before launching.

Maybe you just read our breakdown of [why funnels fail](/learning-center/why-is-my-funnel-not-converting) and realized your funnel has content problems. Now you're asking: what will it cost to fix this?

## The Four Paths (And What Each One Actually Costs)

There are four ways to get a funnel built. Each has a different price tag, timeline, and outcome. Let's go through them one by one.

---

### Path 1: Do It Yourself

**Out-of-Pocket Cost: $100 to $500**
**Time Investment: 40 to 120 hours**
**Timeline: 4 to 12 weeks**

This is the path most people start with. You buy a funnel builder subscription (ClickFunnels, Kajabi, Kartra, or similar), you watch some YouTube videos, and you start writing pages.

Here is what that $100 to $500 covers:
- Funnel builder software: $97 to $297/month
- Stock photos or Canva Pro: $0 to $15/month
- Email service provider (if not included): $0 to $50/month
- Maybe a course on funnel copywriting: $100 to $500 one-time

The real cost is not the subscriptions. The real cost is your time.

If you are running a coaching or consulting business that generates $5,000 to $15,000 per month, your time is worth somewhere between $100 and $300 per hour (conservatively). Building a funnel from scratch, if you have never done it before, will take you 40 to 120 hours. That includes:

- Learning the funnel framework (Perfect Webinar, VSL, whatever model you choose)
- Writing your registration page
- Outlining and scripting your webinar or VSL
- Writing your four-page sales letter
- Writing 8 to 15 emails
- Creating ad copy for multiple platforms
- Recording and editing video (if doing a webinar)
- Setting up the tech and integrations
- Testing everything

At $150/hour, 80 hours of your time equals $12,000 in opportunity cost. That is the number nobody talks about when they say "just do it yourself."

**When This Path Makes Sense:**
- You are pre-revenue or under $3K/month and cannot afford to pay someone
- You genuinely enjoy writing and want to learn the skill for future funnels
- You have the time and are not giving up paid client work to do this

**When This Path Does Not Make Sense:**
- Your time is worth more than $100/hour and you are booked with clients
- You have tried before and your copy does not convert
- You need to launch fast (you cannot afford 8 weeks of build time)

### Path 2: Hire a Funnel Copywriter

**Out-of-Pocket Cost: $3,500 to $15,000**
**Time Investment (your time): 10 to 20 hours**
**Timeline: 3 to 8 weeks**

This is the professional path. You hire someone who knows funnel frameworks, understands your market, and writes high-converting copy for a living.

Here is the typical pricing breakdown for a full funnel (webinar or VSL-based):

**Entry-Level Copywriter ($3,500 to $6,000):**
- Less than 2 years of experience
- May or may not have funnel-specific training
- You will need to provide a detailed creative brief
- Revisions are usually limited (2 rounds max)
- Deliverables: registration page, webinar outline or VSL script, sales page, 8 to 12 emails

**Mid-Level Copywriter ($6,000 to $10,000):**
- 2 to 5 years of experience
- Has worked on successful funnels before
- Conducts customer research and interviews
- Includes strategic consultation (not just execution)
- Deliverables: everything above plus ad copy, multiple headline options, strategic brief
- Revisions: 3 to 4 rounds

**Expert-Level Copywriter ($10,000 to $15,000+):**
- 5+ years, proven track record
- Deep expertise in your specific niche (health, finance, B2B, etc.)
- Full research process, competitive analysis, voice-of-customer mining
- Strategic partner, not just a vendor
- Deliverables: complete funnel ecosystem, multiple variations for testing
- Revisions: as many as needed to hit performance benchmarks

**What This Does NOT Include:**
- Design and layout (you will need a designer or page builder templates)
- Video production (if doing a webinar, you still record and edit it)
- Ads management (the copywriter writes the ads, you or an ads manager runs them)
- Funnel builder software subscription (still your cost)

Not sure whether a copywriter is the right move for you? We wrote a [detailed comparison of hiring a copywriter vs. using an AI tool](/learning-center/copywriter-vs-ai-tool) to help you decide.

**When This Path Makes Sense:**
- You are doing $10K+ per month and can afford the investment
- You have a validated offer (people have bought it before, even in small numbers)
- You want professional-level copy but do not need full-service strategy and ads management
- You are willing to wait 4 to 8 weeks for the first draft

**When This Path Does Not Make Sense:**
- Your offer is not validated yet (a great copywriter cannot save a bad offer)
- You cannot articulate your positioning, audience, and unique mechanism clearly
- You need multiple funnels and cannot afford $10K per funnel
- You are pre-revenue and do not have the capital

### Path 3: Hire a Funnel Agency

**Out-of-Pocket Cost: $15,000 to $50,000+**
**Time Investment (your time): 15 to 30 hours**
**Timeline: 6 to 12 weeks**

This is the full-service path. The agency handles strategy, copy, design, tech setup, and sometimes ads management.

Here is what you get for $15K to $50K:

**Strategy & Research:**
- Market analysis and competitive research
- Customer avatar development
- Offer positioning and pricing strategy
- Funnel architecture (which pages, what sequence, upsells, downsells)

**Copywriting:**
- Full funnel copy (registration, webinar/VSL, sales pages, emails, ads)
- Multiple variations for A/B testing
- Voice-of-customer research and messaging frameworks

**Design & Development:**
- Custom page design (not templates)
- Video editing (if applicable)
- Mobile optimization
- Tech integrations (email, CRM, payment processor, webinar platform)

**Launch Support:**
- Pre-launch checklist and QA
- Sometimes included: initial ad spend management for 30 to 60 days

**When This Path Makes Sense:**
- You are doing $50K+ per month and this is a major launch
- You want a true done-for-you experience
- You have a complex offer (high-ticket, multiple payment plans, upsells, continuity)
- You need someone to own the entire project, not just one piece

**When This Path Does Not Make Sense:**
- You are under $25K/month in revenue (ROI is too hard to justify)
- You want to iterate quickly (agencies move slower because everything is custom)
- You have strong opinions about messaging and do not want to delegate strategy

## The Cost Nobody Talks About: Delay

Here is the cost that does not show up on any invoice but kills more businesses than bad copy ever will: the cost of waiting.

Let's say you have an offer that could reasonably generate $10,000 per month if you had a working funnel driving traffic to it. Every month you delay building that funnel is $10,000 you are not making.

If you choose the DIY path and it takes you 3 months to finish, that is $30,000 in lost revenue. If you choose the agency path and it takes 3 months for them to deliver (plus another month for revisions), that is $40,000 in opportunity cost.

Speed is a feature. The faster you can get a working funnel live, the faster you can start testing, learning, and iterating.

This does not mean you should rush and launch something broken. It means you should pick the path that gets you to "good enough to test" as fast as possible, then improve it based on real data.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I start with a cheaper path and upgrade later?

Yes, and most people do. Start with what you can afford now, launch, and reinvest revenue into better copy or design later. A decent funnel live today beats a perfect funnel 6 months from now.

### What if I hire a copywriter and the funnel does not convert?

This is a real risk. Copywriters write based on the information you give them. If your offer is not validated, your positioning is unclear, or your traffic is cold, even great copy will struggle. Before hiring anyone, make sure you have sold your offer successfully at least a few times (even manually, in DMs or sales calls). That proves the offer works and gives the copywriter something to build from.

### Are AI-generated funnels as good as human-written ones?

Depends on the tool and how you use it. Generic ChatGPT output is not going to compete with a skilled copywriter. But AI tools trained on specific frameworks (like Funnel Studio, which uses Brunson, Cardone, Edwards, and others) produce strategically sound first drafts that you can refine. Think of it like having a junior copywriter who knows the frameworks but needs your stories, voice, and examples added in.

### How much should I budget for ad spend on top of the funnel build cost?

Plan for at least $1,000 to test. Ideally $3,000 to $5,000 if you can afford it. You need enough data to know what is working. If your ad budget is under $500, you are guessing, not testing.

### What is the ROI timeline? When should I expect the funnel to pay for itself?

If the funnel is working, you should see positive ROI within 90 days. That does not mean you are profitable on day one (most funnels are not), but within 3 months you should know whether the funnel converts and what your cost-per-acquisition looks like. If you are 6 months in and still not breaking even, the issue is likely the offer, the audience, or the traffic source, not the funnel copy.

### Can I use a funnel template instead of paying for custom copy?

You can, and templates work if you know how to adapt them to your offer. The problem is most people fill in templates like a form (swap the placeholders, change the product name) and end up with generic copy that does not connect. If you go the template route, treat it like a structure guide, not a fill-in-the-blank worksheet. Rewrite it in your voice, add your stories, and customize the objections to your audience.
